THE MODEL INPUT

STEP 1 - DATA PREPROCESSING FOR SUSCEPTIBILITY MAPPING

Most landslide-prone regions on earth are mountainous, composed by coarse soil and/or lack vegetation which helps anchoring the soil with their roots, or have high moisture content due to slope orientation or proximity to water bodies. All these key elements are environmental factors that can be used to model and estimate landslide susceptibility for a given area. Landslide inventories from competent authorities are also useful for indentifying areas that have already been affected by this natural phenomena. A model for landslide susceptibility mapping and exposure assessment has been implemented for the Tirano region in Lombardy, northern Italy, in a series of steps described in this section.

Input Layers

DTM

The Digital Terrain Model is a raster layer geospatial feature that provides gridded information of the Earth surface height, excluding vegetation and built features. For this case, a DTM of 5mx5m pixel resolution was used, and several layers where computed trough it’s derivatives like Slope, Aspect and Curvature.

SLOPE

calculated by comparing a certain point within a raster to that point's surrounding neighbor’s height values, describing its steepness, incline, or grade. A higher slope value indicates a steeper incline.

Aspect

Refers to the compass direction that a hillside or slope faces, often important in determining the amount of sunlight striking the land's surface, related to vegetation growth, soil moisture, among others.

Plan curvature

Refers to the second derivative along the direction of the slope, to understand whether the terrain curvature is concave or convex.

Profile curvature

Refers to the second derivative along the perpendicular direction of the slope, to understand whether the terrain curvature is concave or convex.

Land Use - DUSAF

Destinazione d'Uso dei Suoli Agricoli e Forestali – DUSAF, a raster layer geospatial feature containing land use destination, characteristics and vegetation cover of the Lombardy Region realized by the Italian ERSAF (Ente Regionale per i Servizi all'Agricoltura e Foreste).

NDVI

Normalized Difference Vegetation Index – NDVI, a widely used index derived by map algebra expression involving the visible red and near infrared (NIR) band. It is a simple, but effective index for quantifying green vegetation. The NDVI normalizes green leaf scattering in the NIR wavelength (Sentinel 2 - Band 8) and chlorophyll absorption in the Red wavelength (Sentinel 2 - Band 4).

Distance to Features

A series of buffer distances from the corresponding lines depicting the Roads, Faults and Rivers of the working region. Distances were set to be 50, 100, 200, 500 m and over 500 m (9999 value), creating a raster layer of the different buffers for each line attribute.
