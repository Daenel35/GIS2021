About our Project

Landslides are one of the most common and harmful natural disaster in the world, accounting for high economic burden due to damages to infrastructure and high number of human fatalities. Different classification has been stablished for understanding the nature of landslides, involving criteria such as the landslide movement type and the type of material involved. Most landslide-prone regions on earth are mountainous, composed by coarse soil and/or lack vegetation which helps anchoring the soil with their roots, or have high moisture content due to slope orientation or proximity to water bodies. All these key elements are environmental factors that can be used to model and estimate landslide susceptibility for a given area. Exposure assessment involves comparing the model data to existing infrastructure and population location, aimed to manage decision making for disaster reduction and hazard risk management. A model for landslide susceptibility mapping and exposure assessment has been implemented for the Tirano region in Lombardy, northern Italy, within the framework of the Geographical Information Systems â€“ GIS 2021/2022 course at Politecnico di Milano, and the results are shared in an interactive web map supported by a webGIS. For more in-depth details on how the model is implemented, click on the know more button.

In order to predict the landslide susceptibility areas of the assigned region it was necessary to carry out a set of steps to process a group of inputs, including the mentioned environmental factors. Those steps have covered mainly the following aspects:

  • Implementation of basic fixing operations on the input layers like resampling and reclassification of their data
  • Transformation from vector data model to raster data
  • Extraction of information by a sampling technique over the different input layers
This preprocessing stage was the foundation for the implementation of the Random Forest model.
