Results Analysis

After the processing steps detailed in Processing Section we have implemented a Random Forest model (Supervised learning algorithm) in order to classify the Landslide and Non-Landslide zones (LZ and NLZ). Here we will show the different approaches used and their associated results. First we will explain an approach with 1000 points and then another one which used 5000 points.

1000 points approach

In this first trial we have considered a train-test ratio of 70-30% and a total ammount of points of 1000. We have sampled in the following way our points:

  • LZ - Train: 350 points
  • NLZ - Train: 350 points
  • LZ - Test: 150 points
  • NLZ - Test: 150 points

The Overall Accuracy we have reached was 72%


Class NLZ LZ UA PA
NLZ 74 13 85% 52%
LZ 68 136 67% 91%

5000 points approach

  • Train-test Split: 70-30% (the same)
  • Total ammount of points: 5000
  • Was considered the relative distribution of areas between LZ and NLZ in order to distribute the points
Class of area % surface Assigning by proportion Train points Test points
LZ 32,40% 1620 1134 486
NLZ 67,60% 3380 2366 1014
Total 100% 5000 3500 1500

We considered absolutely relevant to try to distribute in an homogeneous way the points to cover in a similar way both classes. Also was important to increment the number of points to cover more areas and in this way have a higher volume of comparisons in order to perform the classification.

Finally we got an accuracy of 80%

NLZ 747 93 89% 79%
LZ 197 388 66% 81%

Final consideration

This project has shown interesting results in the implementation of a Machine Learning Approach by using satellite images in order to determine the Landslide and Non-Landslide zones of Tirano Region. The use of a GIS approach to determine the proposed objective was important considering that it would help policy makers to get information by an indirect method which has huge advantages in terms of costs and velocity of the processing.
